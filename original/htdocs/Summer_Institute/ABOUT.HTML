The Plasma Science and Fusion Energy Institute is a two-week intensive workshop designed to provide the opportunity to study plasma physics and fusion energy through experimental research in our state-of-the-art laboratories. Participants will perform experiments, in collaboration with laboratory scientists, that investigate the basic properties of plasmas. Finally, plasmas are ideal to illustrate many concepts in high school physics curricula including waves, atoms, nuclear reactions, relativity, electricity and magnetism. An integral part of the Institute will be the development of new plasma-based lesson plans, student-led investigations and demonstrations.

Eligibility and Selection

Current physics teachers of grades 9-12 are eligible to apply. Selection is limited to 12 and is based, in part, on the applicant's teaching, research, and educational experience. Applications from those with experience in laboratory research and creation of new and innovative curricula are especially encouraged.

Stipend

Participants will receive a stipend of $600 upon completion of the Institute. Expenses for travel to and from the university and housing will be covered. Commuters will be considered but on-site housing is encouraged.

Topics Covered

Lecture/Discussion

  • Scientific Methodology
  • Energy in a Global Economy
  • Energy and the Environment
  • An Introduction to Plasma Physics
  • An Introduction to Fusion Energy
  • Experimental Plasma Physics
  • Tokamaks
  • Plasma Astrophysics
  • Plasmas as a teaching tool in the classroom

Laboratory

  • Plasma breakdown in glow discharges
  • The effect of magnetic fields on plasma behavior
  • Measurement of plasma temperature and density
  • Plasma spectroscopy
  • Microwave interactions with plasmas

About the Laboratory

The Princeton University Plasma Physics Laboratory is a collaborative national center for plasma and fusion science, devoted to innovation and scientific understanding leading to an attractive fusion energy source.

Its associated mission is to conduct world-class research along the broad frontier of plasma science and provide the highest quality of scientific education.

Our ultimate mission is to create the key innovations which will make fusion energy a practical reality.
